I am a PhD student at Imperial College London specialising in numerical modelling of natural environmental systems. My research combines advanced computational methods with civil engineering principles to simulate and analyse complex environmental processes. I work extensively with numerical modeling frameworks across multiple programming languages including MATLAB, Python, Fortran, and Julia, developing simulations that help us better understand how natural systems respond to various conditions and changes. I have an MEng degree in civil engineering and an MPhil degree in marine environmental science (physical oceanography), and I'm passionate about bridging the gap between theoretical environmental science and practical computational solutions that can inform real-world decision-making.

My doctoral research focuses on developing and implementing advanced numerical models to simulate complex processes in natural environmental systems. My work involves creating robust computational models that can accurately capture the intricate interactions between physical, chemical, and biological processes in environmental systems.
